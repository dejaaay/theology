Events

Youth Retreats

Youth can deepen their faith and take part in activities that foster their spiritual and emotional development at retreats based on Christus Vivit. Team-building exercises, social justice debates, and occasions for prayer and meditation are all possible activities.During these retreats, you can participate in prayer and worship, listen to talks and testimonies, and engage in group discussions and activities. You can reflect on how your faith can guide your choices and actions as a young person in today's world.

Environmental stewardship workshops

Participants can learn about environmental stewardship and the significance of defending and conserving our planet by attending workshops centered on the principles of Laudato Si. Learning about composting, planting trees, and decreasing waste are some possible activities. You can learn practical ways to reduce your environmental footprint, such as reducing waste, conserving energy, and supporting sustainable practices. You can also reflect on how our actions impact the environment and the people who depend on it.

Social justice campaigns

Campaigns for social justice that emphasize the concepts of all three papers might aid in bringing attention to pressing concerns and motivating people to take action. Campaigns could help oppressed groups, promote youth empowerment, or fight for environmental conservation, as examples. You can learn about the Catholic Church's teachings on social justice, including the principles of solidarity and subsidiarity. You can also learn how to advocate for policies that promote justice, equality, and dignity for all.

Interfaith dialogue events

Events promoting interfaith dialogue might bring members of many religions together to talk about issues raised in the texts, such as protecting the environment or promoting social justice. You can learn about the Catholic Church's teachings on interfaith relations, including the importance of dialogue and mutual respect. You can also reflect on how your faith informs your relationships with people of other religions.

Holy Week

Holy Week is a time of reflection and renewal for Catholics. You can use this time to deepen your understanding of the Catholic Church's teachings on the passion, death, and resurrection of Jesus Christ. You can participate in Holy Week liturgies, such as Palm Sunday, Holy Thursday, Good Friday, and Easter Vigil. You can also reflect on how the events of Holy Week relate to your own life and faith journey. By participating in Holy Week, you can deepen your faith and connect with the core teachings of the Church. This can include attending Mass, participating in the Stations of the Cross, and practicing fasting and prayer.

Laudato Si

The encyclical has been widely praised for its strong stance on environmental issues, particularly climate change. It has been hailed as a call to action for Catholics and non-Catholics alike to care for the planet and its inhabitants. The release of Laudato Si was accompanied by a number of events, including a press conference with Cardinal Peter Turkson and Bishop Mario Toso, the head of the Pontifical Council for Justice and Peace, and a special prayer service at St. Peter's Basilica. The encyclical has been the subject of numerous conferences, seminars, and discussions around the world. It has also been incorporated into the curriculum of Catholic schools and universities.

Gaudete et Exsultate

The apostolic exhortation, which focuses on the call to holiness in the modern world, has generated significant discussion about what it means to be holy and how one can strive for holiness in daily life. The document has been praised for its accessibility and relevance to laypeople, as well as its emphasis on the importance of social justice and the protection of human dignity. In 2018, Pope Francis held a special event at the Vatican to mark the publication of Gaudete et Exsultate. The event featured talks and discussions about the themes of the exhortation, as well as a prayer service led by the pope.

Christus Vivit

The apostolic exhortation, which addresses the challenges facing young people today, has been widely discussed in the context of the Catholic Church's efforts to engage with young people and to address the issues that affect them. The document has been praised for its pastoral tone and its recognition of the unique challenges facing young people in the modern world. It has also been criticized by some for not going far enough in addressing issues such as gender and sexuality. In 2019, Pope Francis held a special meeting with young people at the Vatican to discuss the themes of Christus Vivit. The meeting included discussions about issues such as migration, the environment, and the role of women in the Church.
